Proof

We only show that if \(v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n\) are linearly independent then \(v_1^\prime,v_2^\prime,\ldots,v_n^\prime\) are linearly independent. Suppose that there are \(\gamma_i\in F\) such that
\begin{equation*} \gamma_1 v_1^\prime+\cdots+\gamma_{n-1}v_{n-1}^\prime+\gamma_nv_n^\prime=0, \end{equation*}
i.e.,
\begin{equation*} \gamma_1 v_1+\cdots+\gamma_{n-1}v_{n-1}+\gamma_n (v_n+\alpha v_1)=0 \end{equation*}
Because \(v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n\) are linearly independent, we get that
\begin{equation*} \gamma_1+\gamma_n\alpha=\gamma_2=\gamma_3=\cdots=\gamma_n=0. \end{equation*}
Hence all \(\gamma_i=0\) and \(v_1^\prime,v_2^\prime,\ldots,v_n^\prime\) are linearly independent.
