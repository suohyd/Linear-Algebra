Definition 7.3.1. (Algebraic multiplicity).

Let \(\chi_T\) be the characteristic polynomial of \(T\) and let \(\lambda\in F\) be an eigenvalue of \(T\text{.}\) Then, by the repeated application of LemmaÂ A.1.6, we can write \(\chi_T=(t-\lambda)^m\cdot q(t)\in F[t]\) with \(q(\lambda)\neq 0\text{.}\) The natural number \(m\) is said to be the algebraic multiplicity of \(\lambda\).
