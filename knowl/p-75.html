Suppose that there exists \(\alpha_{ij},\beta_i\in F\) such that
\begin{equation*} \sum_{i\neq j}\alpha_{ij}E_{ij}+\sum_{i\neq 1}\beta_i(E_{11}-E_{ii})=0. \end{equation*}
In other words the above equation is
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix}\sum_{i=2}^{n}\beta_i\amp\alpha_{12}\amp\alpha_{13}\amp\cdots\amp\alpha_{1n}\\\alpha_{21}\amp-\beta_2\amp\alpha_{23}\amp\cdots\amp\alpha_{2n}\\\vdots\amp\vdots\amp\vdots\amp\ddots\amp\vdots\\\alpha_{n1}\amp\alpha_{n2}\amp\alpha_{n3}\amp\cdots\amp -\beta_n\end{pmatrix}=0. \end{equation*}
Hence, \(\alpha_{ij}=0,\beta_i=0\text{.}\) We thus get \(\{E_{ij}:i\neq j\}\cup\{E_{11}-E_{ii}:2\leq i\leq n\}\) is linearly independent.
