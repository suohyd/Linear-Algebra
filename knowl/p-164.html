Let \(\lambda\in F\) be a root of \(\chi_T\text{,}\) i.e., \(\lambda\) is an eigenvalue of \(T\) (see CorollaryÂ 7.2.14). Let \(v\in V\) be an eigenvector corresponding to \(\lambda\text{.}\) Thus \(T^k(v)=\lambda^kv\) for any \(k\in\mathbb{N}\text{.}\) Suppose that \(m_T=a_0+a_1t+\cdots+a_{r-1}t^{r-1}+t^r\in F[t]\text{.}\) Since \(m_T\) is an annihilating polynomial we get the following.
\begin{equation*} 0=m_T(T)(v)=a_0v+a_1\lambda v+\cdots+a_{r-1}\lambda^{r-1}v+\lambda^rv=m_T(\lambda)v \end{equation*}
As \(v\) is an eigenvector, it is nonzero. Hence we must have \(m_T(\lambda)=0\text{,}\) i.e., \(\lambda\) is a root of \(m_T\text{.}\)
