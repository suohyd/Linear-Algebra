There exists scalars \(\alpha_i\in F\text{,}\) not all zero, such that
\begin{equation*} \alpha_1 v_1+\cdots+\alpha_n v_n+\alpha_{n+1}v_{n+1}=0. \end{equation*}
If \(\alpha_{n+1}=0\) then the linear independence of \(v_1,v_2,\ldots,v_n\) implies that \(\alpha_1=\alpha_2=\cdots=\alpha_n=0\text{,}\) which will be a contradiction. Hence, \(\alpha_{n+1}\neq 0\text{.}\) Therefore,
\begin{equation*} v_{n+1}=\tfrac{\alpha_1}{\alpha_{n+1}}v_1+\cdots+\tfrac{\alpha_n}{\alpha_{n+1}}v_n. \end{equation*}
