Consider an \(\R\)-linear map \(T\) from \(\R^3\) to itself given by \(e_1\mapsto e_2\text{,}\) \(e_2\mapsto -e_1\text{,}\) and \(e_3\mapsto e_3\text{.}\) The characteristic polynomial of \(T\) is \(\chi_T=(t-1)(t^2+1)\in\R[t]\) and it has only one root in \(\R\text{.}\) Hence \(\chi_T\) not split over \(\R\text{.}\) By TheoremÂ 7.4.4, \(T\) is not triangulable.
