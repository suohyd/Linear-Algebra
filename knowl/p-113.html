Thus, the matrix \(AT_{pq}(\alpha)\) is obtained by multiplying the \(q\)-th column of \(A\) by \(\alpha\in F\) and adding this column to the \(p\)-th column of \(A\text{.}\) Therefore, using notation of eq. (5.4.2), the column space of \(AT_{pq}(\alpha)\) is
\begin{equation*} \big\langle Af_1^t,\ldots,Af_{p-1}^t,Af_p^t+\alpha Af_q^t,Af_{p+1}^t,\ldots,Af_q^t,\ldots,Af_n^t\big\rangle. \end{equation*}
Since, \(Af_p^t=(Af_p^t+\alpha Af_q^t)-\alpha Af_q^t\) we get
\begin{equation*} \big\langle Af_1^t,\ldots,Af_{p-1}^t,Af_p^t+\alpha Af_q^t,Af_{p+1}^t,\ldots,Af_q^t,Af_n^t\big\rangle=\langle Af_i^t:1\leq i\leq n\rangle. \end{equation*}
We thus have \(crk(A)=crk(AT_{pq}(\alpha))\text{.}\)
