Example 8.3.5.

Let \(A=\begin{pmatrix}1\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\\0\amp 1\amp 0\amp 0\\-2\amp -2\amp 0\amp 1\\-2\amp 0\amp -1\amp -2\end{pmatrix}\in M_4(\C)\text{.}\) We find its Jordan form.
  1. Eigenvalues of \(A\) are \(1,1,-1,-1\text{.}\) Thus the algebraic multiplicity of both \(1\) and \(-1\) is \(2\text{.}\)
  2. To find geometric multiplicity of \(1\) and \(-1\text{.}\) We have
    \begin{equation*} A-I_4=\begin{pmatrix}0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\\0\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\\-2\amp -2\amp -1\amp 1\\-2\amp 0\amp -1\amp -3\end{pmatrix}\quad\text{and}\quad A+I_4=\begin{pmatrix}2\amp 0\amp 0\amp 0\\0\amp 2\amp 0\amp 0\\-2\amp -2\amp 1\amp 1\\-2\amp 0\amp -1\amp -1\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}
    Thus, nullity of \(A-I_4\text{,}\) i.e., the geometric multiplicity of \(\lambda=1\) is \(2\text{.}\) Hence, the number of Jordan blocks corresponding to \(\lambda=1\) is \(2\text{.}\) On the other hand, computing the row-reduced echelon form of \(A+I_4\text{,}\) we get that the nullity of \(A+I_4\) is \(1\text{.}\) Hence the geometric multiplicity of \(\lambda=-1\) is \(1\text{.}\) Therefore, the number of Jordan block corresponding to \(\lambda=-1\) is \(1\text{.}\) Thus \(A\) is similar to
    \begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix}1\amp \amp \amp \\\amp 1\amp \amp \\\amp \amp -1\amp \\\amp \amp 1\amp -1\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}
