Proof

Suppose that \(A\) and \(B\) be matrix representations of \(T\) with respect to some bases of \(V\text{.}\) Then there exists an invertible matrix \(P\) such that \(B=C^{-1}AC\) (see CorollaryÂ 5.3.6). Thus,
\begin{equation*} tI_n-B=tI_n-C^{-1}AC=C^{-1}(tI_n-A)C. \end{equation*}
Hence
\begin{equation*} \det(tI_n-B)=\det\left(C^{-1}(tI_n-A)C\right)=\det(tI_n-A). \end{equation*}
Thus the result is proved.
