Remark 3.1.2.

  1. Any subset of \(V\) containing the zero vector is linearly dependent.
  2. If \(v\) and \(w\) are linearly dependent then, there exists a nonzero \(\alpha\) (resp., a nonzero \(\beta\)) such that \(v=\alpha w\) (resp., \(w=\beta v\)). In other words, \(v\) and \(w\) lie on the same line (refer Definition 2.5.15) and hence \(v,w\) are collinear.
  3. The relation ’\(v\) and \(w\) are linearly dependent’ is an equivalence relation on the set of nonzero vectors of \(V\) (Verify this statement). The equivalence classes are lines with zero removed.
