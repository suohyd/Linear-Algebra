Item 2.

The geometric multiplicity of \(2\) is the dimension of the kernel of \(A-2I_2=\begin{pmatrix}3\amp 4\amp 1\\-1\amp -2\amp 0\\-3\amp -4\amp -1\end{pmatrix}\text{.}\) The row-reduced echelon form of this matrix is
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix}1\amp 0\amp 1\\0\amp 1\amp -1/2\\0\amp 0\amp 0\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}
Therefore, the geometric multiplicity of \(\lambda=2\) is \(1\text{.}\) Hence the number of Jordan blocks corresponding to \(\lambda=2\) is \(1\text{.}\) Hence, \(A\) must be similar to
\begin{equation*} \begin{pmatrix}2\amp 0\amp 0\\1\amp 2\amp 0\\0\amp 1\amp 2\end{pmatrix}. \end{equation*}
